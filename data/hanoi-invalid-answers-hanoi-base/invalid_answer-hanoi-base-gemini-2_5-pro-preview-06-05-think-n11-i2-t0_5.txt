### Thinking Process and Strategy

The problem is to move a stack of $n$ disks from a source peg to a destination peg using an auxiliary peg, following the given rules. Let's denote the pegs as `source`, `destination`, and `auxiliary`.

The recursive strategy for moving $n$ disks from `source` to `destination` is as follows:

1.  **Move $n-1$ disks from `source` to `auxiliary`**: This gets the top $n-1$ disks out of the way, freeing the largest disk ($n$). The `destination` peg is used as the temporary peg for this sub-problem.
2.  **Move the largest disk ($n$) from `source` to `destination`**: This is a single, direct move.
3.  **Move the $n-1$ disks from `auxiliary` to `destination`**: This places the smaller disks on top of the largest disk ($n$). The `source` peg is now used as the temporary peg.

The base case for the recursion is when we need to move only one disk ($n=1$). In that case, we simply move it directly from its `source` to its `destination`.

For your specific problem:
-   Number of disks ($n$): 11
-   Source peg: 0
-   Destination peg: 2
-   Auxiliary peg: 1

The top-level call to our recursive function would be `move_stack(disks=11, source=0, destination=2, auxiliary=1)`.

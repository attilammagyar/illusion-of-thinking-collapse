Of course. This is a classic puzzle known as the Tower of Hanoi. The solution can be found using a straightforward recursive algorithm.

### Strategy

To move a stack of $n$ disks from a source peg to a destination peg using an auxiliary peg, we can break the problem down as follows:

1.  Move the top $n-1$ disks from the **source** peg to the **auxiliary** peg.
2.  Move the single largest disk (disk $n$) from the **source** peg to the **destination** peg.
3.  Move the $n-1$ disks from the **auxiliary** peg to the **destination** peg.

This process is repeated recursively until we are only moving a single disk. For $n=11$ disks, this will require $2^{11} - 1 = 2047$ moves.

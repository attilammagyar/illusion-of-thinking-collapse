### Method

To move a stack of $n$ disks from a source peg to a destination peg using an auxiliary peg, we can follow these three steps:
1.  Move the top $n-1$ disks from the **source** peg to the **auxiliary** peg.
2.  Move the single largest disk (disk $n$) from the **source** peg to the **destination** peg.
3.  Move the $n-1$ disks from the **auxiliary** peg to the **destination** peg.

For your specific problem:
-   **n**: 10 disks
-   **Source Peg**: 0
-   **Destination Peg**: 2
-   **Auxiliary Peg**: 1

The total number of moves required for $n$ disks is $2^n - 1$. For 10 disks, this is $2^{10} - 1 = 1024 - 1 = 1023$ moves.

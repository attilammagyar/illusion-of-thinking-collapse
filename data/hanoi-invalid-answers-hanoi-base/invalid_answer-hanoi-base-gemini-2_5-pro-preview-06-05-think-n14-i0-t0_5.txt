### The Strategy

The core idea is to break the problem down into smaller, similar problems:

To move **n** disks from a **source** peg to a **target** peg:
1.  Move **n-1** disks from the **source** peg to the **auxiliary** peg.
2.  Move the single largest disk (**n**) from the **source** peg to the **target** peg.
3.  Move the **n-1** disks from the **auxiliary** peg to the **target** peg.

This process is repeated until we are only moving the smallest disk (the base case of the recursion).

For your specific puzzle with 14 disks, we want to move them from peg 0 to peg 2, using peg 1 as the auxiliary peg. The total number of moves required will be $2^{14} - 1$, which is **16,383**.

### Strategy

To move $n$ disks from a source peg to a destination peg:
1.  Move $n-1$ disks from the source peg to the auxiliary peg.
2.  Move the $n$-th (largest) disk from the source peg to the destination peg.
3.  Move the $n-1$ disks from the auxiliary peg to the destination peg.

For this problem with $n=11$ disks, the pegs are:
-   **Source**: Peg 0
-   **Destination**: Peg 2
-   **Auxiliary**: Peg 1

The total number of moves required for $n$ disks is $2^n - 1$. For 11 disks, this is $2^{11} - 1 = 2048 - 1 = 2047$ moves.

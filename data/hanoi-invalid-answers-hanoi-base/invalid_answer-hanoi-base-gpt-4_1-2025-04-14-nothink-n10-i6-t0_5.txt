This is the classic **Tower of Hanoi** puzzle with 10 disks.

### Solution Approach

The optimal solution uses recursion and always follows this pattern for `n` disks from peg `src` to peg `dst` using `aux` as auxiliary:

1. Move `n-1` disks from `src` to `aux` (using `dst` as auxiliary).
2. Move the largest disk (`n`) from `src` to `dst`.
3. Move `n-1` disks from `aux` to `dst` (using `src` as auxiliary).

Each move is recorded as `[disk id, from peg, to peg]`.

moves = []
def hanoi(n, src, dst, aux):
    if n == 0:
        return
    hanoi(n-1, src, aux, dst)
    moves.append([n, src, dst])
    hanoi(n-1, aux, dst, src)
hanoi(10, 0, 2, 1)

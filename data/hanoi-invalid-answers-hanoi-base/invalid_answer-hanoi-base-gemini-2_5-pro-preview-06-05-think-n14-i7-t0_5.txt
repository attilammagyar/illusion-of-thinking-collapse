### Thinking Process and Solution Strategy

The problem is to move a stack of $n$ disks from a source peg to a destination peg using an auxiliary peg, following the given rules. For this specific problem, we have:
- $n = 14$ disks
- Source peg: 0
- Destination peg: 2
- Auxiliary peg: 1

The recursive strategy to solve this is as follows:
1.  Move the top $n-1$ disks (disks 1 to 13) from the `source` peg (0) to the `auxiliary` peg (1).
2.  Move the largest disk ($n$, which is disk 14) from the `source` peg (0) to the `destination` peg (2).
3.  Move the $n-1$ disks (disks 1 to 13) from the `auxiliary` peg (1) to the `destination` peg (2).

This process is applied recursively. The base case for the recursion is when we need to move only one disk ($n=1$), which is a single move from its source to its destination.

The total number of moves required for $n$ disks is $2^n - 1$. For 14 disks, this will be $2^{14} - 1 = 16384 - 1 = 16,383$ moves.

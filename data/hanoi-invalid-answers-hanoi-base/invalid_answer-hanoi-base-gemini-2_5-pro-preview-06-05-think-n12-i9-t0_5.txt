### Thinking Process and Algorithm

The problem is to move $n$ disks from a source peg to a destination peg using an auxiliary peg. For your request, we have:
- $n = 12$ disks
- Source peg = 0
- Destination peg = 2
- Auxiliary peg = 1

The recursive strategy is as follows:
To move $n$ disks from a `source` peg to a `destination` peg:
1.  If $n > 0$:
    a. Move $n-1$ disks from the `source` peg to the `auxiliary` peg.
    b. Move the largest disk, disk $n$, from the `source` peg to the `destination` peg.
    c. Move the $n-1$ disks from the `auxiliary` peg to the `destination` peg.

This process breaks the large problem down into smaller, identical subproblems until we are only moving a single disk.

For $n=12$, this will require $2^{12} - 1 = 4096 - 1 = 4095$ moves.

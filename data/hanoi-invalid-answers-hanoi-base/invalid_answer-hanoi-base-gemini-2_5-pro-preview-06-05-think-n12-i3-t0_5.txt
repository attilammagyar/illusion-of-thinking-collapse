### The Algorithm

To move a stack of $n$ disks from a source peg to a destination peg using an auxiliary peg, we can break it down into three steps:

1.  Move the top $n-1$ disks from the **source** peg to the **auxiliary** peg.
2.  Move the single largest disk ($n$) from the **source** peg to the **destination** peg.
3.  Move the $n-1$ disks from the **auxiliary** peg to the **destination** peg.

This process is repeated until we are only moving a single disk (the base case of the recursion). For 12 disks, this will require $2^{12} - 1 = 4096 - 1 = 4095$ moves.

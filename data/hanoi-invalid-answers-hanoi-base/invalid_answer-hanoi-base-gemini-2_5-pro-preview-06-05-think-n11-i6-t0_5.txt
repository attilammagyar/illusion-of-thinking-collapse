Of course. This is a classic puzzle known as the Tower of Hanoi. The solution can be found using a recursive algorithm.

### The Strategy

To move a stack of $n$ disks from a source peg to a target peg, we can follow these three steps:

1.  Move the top $n-1$ disks from the **source** peg to the **auxiliary** peg.
2.  Move the single largest disk (disk $n$) from the **source** peg to the **target** peg.
3.  Move the $n-1$ disks from the **auxiliary** peg to the **target** peg.

This process is repeated recursively until we are only moving a single disk.

For your specific problem with $n=11$ disks:
- **Source Peg:** 0
- **Target Peg:** 2
- **Auxiliary Peg:** 1

The total number of moves required for $n$ disks is $2^n - 1$. For 11 disks, this will be $2^{11} - 1 = 2048 - 1 = \textbf{2047}$ moves.

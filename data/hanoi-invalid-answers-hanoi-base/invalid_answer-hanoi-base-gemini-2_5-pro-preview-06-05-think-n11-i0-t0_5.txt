### The Strategy

The core idea is to break the problem down into smaller, similar problems. To move a stack of $n$ disks from a source peg to a destination peg, we can follow these three steps:

1.  Move the top $n-1$ disks from the **source** peg to the **auxiliary** (temporary) peg.
2.  Move the single largest disk (disk $n$) from the **source** peg to the **destination** peg.
3.  Move the $n-1$ disks from the **auxiliary** peg to the **destination** peg.

This process is repeated until we are only moving a single disk (the base case of the recursion).

For your specific problem with 11 disks:
-   **n**: 11
-   **Source Peg**: 0
-   **Destination Peg**: 2
-   **Auxiliary Peg**: 1

The total number of moves required for $n$ disks is $2^n - 1$. For 11 disks, this is $2^{11} - 1 = 2048 - 1 = \textbf{2047}$ moves.

### The Strategy

To move a stack of $n$ disks from a source peg to a target peg, we can use an auxiliary peg and follow these three steps:
1.  Move the top $n-1$ disks from the **source** peg to the **auxiliary** peg.
2.  Move the single largest disk ($n$) from the **source** peg to the **target** peg.
3.  Move the $n-1$ disks from the **auxiliary** peg to the **target** peg.

For your puzzle with 12 disks, this means:
-   `n = 12`
-   Source peg: 0
-   Target peg: 2
-   Auxiliary peg: 1

The total number of moves required for $n$ disks is $2^n - 1$. For 12 disks, this is $2^{12} - 1 = 4096 - 1 = \textbf{4095}$ moves.

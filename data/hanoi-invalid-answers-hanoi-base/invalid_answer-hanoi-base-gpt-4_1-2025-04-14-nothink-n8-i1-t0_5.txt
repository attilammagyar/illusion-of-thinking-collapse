Let's solve the Tower of Hanoi puzzle for **8 disks**.

### **Approach**

The classic recursive solution for the Tower of Hanoi puzzle is:

- Move \( n-1 \) disks from source to auxiliary peg.
- Move the largest disk (disk \( n \)) from source to target peg.
- Move \( n-1 \) disks from auxiliary to target peg.

Let's define a function:

```
hanoi(n, source, target, auxiliary, moves)
```
- `n`: the number of disks to move (disk numbers 1..n, 1 is smallest)
- `source`: the starting peg (0)
- `target`: the target peg (2)
- `auxiliary`: the helper peg (1)
- `moves`: the list to store moves

Each move is represented as `[disk id, from peg, to peg]`.

### **Python-like Pseudocode**

```python
def hanoi(n, source, target, auxiliary, moves):
    if n == 1:
        moves.append([1, source, target])
    else:
        hanoi(n-1, source, auxiliary, target, moves)
        moves.append([n, source, target])
        hanoi(n-1, auxiliary, target, source, moves)
```

### The Strategy

The core idea is to break the problem down into smaller, identical problems:

To move **n** disks from a **source** peg to a **target** peg:
1.  Move **n-1** disks from the **source** peg to the **auxiliary** peg.
2.  Move the single largest disk (**n**) from the **source** peg to the **target** peg.
3.  Move the **n-1** disks from the **auxiliary** peg to the **target** peg.

For your specific problem with 10 disks:
- **n**: 10
- **Source Peg**: 0
- **Target Peg**: 2
- **Auxiliary Peg**: 1

The process will be:
1.  Move 9 disks (1-9) from peg 0 to peg 1.
2.  Move the largest disk (10) from peg 0 to peg 2.
3.  Move the 9 disks from peg 1 to peg 2.

This recursive process continues until we are only moving a single disk. The total number of moves for *n* disks is 2ⁿ - 1, which for 10 disks is 2¹⁰ - 1 = **1023 moves**.

### Understanding the Algorithm

To move a stack of $n$ disks from a source peg to a destination peg, we can use an auxiliary (temporary) peg and follow these three steps:

1.  Move the top $n-1$ disks from the **source** peg to the **auxiliary** peg.
2.  Move the single largest disk (disk $n$) from the **source** peg to the **destination** peg.
3.  Move the $n-1$ disks from the **auxiliary** peg to the **destination** peg.

The base case for this recursion is when we need to move only one disk ($n=1$), in which case we just move it directly from the source to the destination.

For your puzzle with $n=14$ disks, the total number of moves required is $2^{14} - 1 = 16,384 - 1 = 16,383$.

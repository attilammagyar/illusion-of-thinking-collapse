### Understanding the Problem

The puzzle described is the classic **Tower of Hanoi** problem. Here's a quick recap of the setup:

- **Pegs**: There are three pegs labeled 0, 1, and 2.
- **Disks**: There are `n` disks (in this case, `n = 10`) of different sizes, numbered from 1 (smallest) to 10 (largest).
- **Initial State**: All disks are stacked on Peg 0 in descending order of size (largest at the bottom, smallest at the top).
- **Goal State**: All disks are moved to Peg 2 in the same order.
- **Rules**:
  1. Only one disk can be moved at a time.
  2. A move consists of taking the top disk from one peg and placing it on top of another peg.
  3. A larger disk cannot be placed on top of a smaller disk.

### General Approach to Solve the Tower of Hanoi

The Tower of Hanoi problem can be solved recursively. The recursive solution is based on the following steps:

1. **Move the top `n-1` disks** from the starting peg (Peg 0) to the auxiliary peg (Peg 1), using the target peg (Peg 2) as a temporary holding area.
2. **Move the largest disk (disk `n`)** from the starting peg (Peg 0) directly to the target peg (Peg 2).
3. **Move the `n-1` disks** from the auxiliary peg (Peg 1) to the target peg (Peg 2), using the starting peg (Peg 0) as a temporary holding area.

This approach reduces the problem of moving `n` disks to moving `n-1` disks, and so on, until we reach the base case of moving a single disk.

### Recursive Solution in Terms of Moves

To generate the sequence of moves, we can implement this recursive strategy. Here's how the moves would be generated for `n` disks:

1. **Base Case**: If `n == 1`, simply move the disk from the starting peg to the target peg.
2. **Recursive Case**:
   - Move `n-1` disks from `start` to `auxiliary` using `target` as the temporary peg.
   - Move the `n`th disk from `start` to `target`.
   - Move `n-1` disks from `auxiliary` to `target` using `start` as the temporary peg.

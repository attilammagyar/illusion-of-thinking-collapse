### Understanding the Problem

The puzzle described is the classic **Tower of Hanoi** problem with 10 disks. The goal is to move all disks from the first peg (Peg 0) to the third peg (Peg 2), following the three rules:

1. Only one disk can be moved at a time.
2. A move consists of taking the top disk from one peg and placing it on top of another peg.
3. A larger disk cannot be placed on top of a smaller disk.

### Recursive Approach

The Tower of Hanoi problem is well-known and has a recursive solution. Here's how the recursive approach works:

1. **Base Case**: If there's only one disk, move it directly from the source peg to the target peg.
2. **Recursive Case**: For `n` disks:
   - Move the top `n-1` disks from the source peg to the auxiliary peg (using the target peg as a temporary peg).
   - Move the `n-th` disk (the largest one) from the source peg to the target peg.
   - Move the `n-1` disks from the auxiliary peg to the target peg (using the source peg as a temporary peg).

This approach ensures that the rules are followed at every step.

### Applying to 10 Disks

For `n = 10`, the sequence of moves can be generated recursively. Here's how the moves would look:

1. Move disks 1 through 9 from Peg 0 to Peg 1 (using Peg 2 as auxiliary).
2. Move disk 10 from Peg 0 to Peg 2.
3. Move disks 1 through 9 from Peg 1 to Peg 2 (using Peg 0 as auxiliary).

Each of these steps (like moving disks 1-9) is broken down further recursively.

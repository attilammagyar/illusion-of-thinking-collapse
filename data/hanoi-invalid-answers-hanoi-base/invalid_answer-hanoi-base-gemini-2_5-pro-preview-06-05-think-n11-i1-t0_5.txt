Of course. This is the classic Tower of Hanoi puzzle. The most efficient way to solve this is with a recursive algorithm.

The strategy to move $n$ disks from a source peg to a destination peg is as follows:
1.  Move the top $n-1$ disks from the source peg to the auxiliary peg.
2.  Move the largest disk, $n$, from the source peg to the destination peg.
3.  Move the $n-1$ disks from the auxiliary peg to the destination peg.

This process is repeated until we are only moving a single disk. For 11 disks, this results in $2^{11} - 1 = 2047$ moves.

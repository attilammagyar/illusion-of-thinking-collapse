### The Strategy

The core idea is to break the problem down into smaller, manageable steps. To move a tower of $n$ disks from a source peg to a target peg, we follow this three-step recursive process:

1.  Move the top $n-1$ disks from the **source** peg to the **auxiliary** peg.
2.  Move the single remaining (largest) disk, disk $n$, from the **source** peg to the **target** peg.
3.  Move the $n-1$ disks from the **auxiliary** peg to the **target** peg.

The "base case" for the recursion is when we only need to move one disk ($n=1$), which is a single, direct move.

For your specific problem with 12 disks:
-   **n**: 12
-   **Source Peg**: 0
-   **Target Peg**: 2
-   **Auxiliary Peg**: 1

The total number of moves required for $n$ disks is $2^n - 1$. For 12 disks, this is $2^{12} - 1 = 4096 - 1 = 4095$ moves.

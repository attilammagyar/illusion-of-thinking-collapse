### Thinking Process and Algorithm

The strategy to move $n$ disks from a source peg to a target peg is as follows:

1.  Move the top $n-1$ disks from the source peg to the auxiliary peg.
2.  Move the single largest disk (disk $n$) from the source peg to the target peg.
3.  Move the $n-1$ disks from the auxiliary peg to the target peg.

The base case for the recursion is moving a single disk ($n=1$), which is a simple one-step move.

For your request with $n=14$ disks, we want to move them from peg 0 to peg 2, using peg 1 as the auxiliary peg. The total number of moves will be $2^{14} - 1 = 16,383$.
